My Letter
My personal experience with these issues stems from the fact that my father is undocumented, which places him at significant risk of deportation. For as long as I can remember, he has made numerous efforts to obtain U.S. citizenship. Despite appearing in court multiple times to resolve his status, he has not yet succeeded. In support of his case, all of my siblings and I have written letters to U.S. Immigration Services, appealing for them to consider granting him citizenship and to prevent his deportation. Till this day, we still have not been able to get him his citizenship. Later this year he has another court date and with all the new orders placed under Trump, this is the scariest court date for my family to go through. Below is the letter I submitted in 2021.
Know Your Rights
Your Rights If you are confronted by a police officer or immigration agent:
  1. You have the right to remain silent and you are not required to answer any questions. You may tell the police officer or immigration agent that you choose to remain silent.
  2. If you have any valid immigration documentation, such as a permanent resident card, an employment authorization card, or a certificate of naturalization, you may show your documentation to an immigration agent if the agent asks to see them.
  3. The officer or agent may pat you down if they believe you are carrying a weapon
If police officers or immigration agents come to your home:
  1. You do not have to open the door if police officers or immigration agents come to your home and do not have a warrant.
  2. If you are unsure if the officer is an immigration agent, you may ask what agency they represent and specifically if they are immigration agents or from ICE
  3. You may ask the officers or agents why they are there.
  4. If the officers or agents say they have a warrant, you may ask to see it before letting them inside.
  5. If the warrant is issued by a court and signed by a judge, the officers may enter your home. If the document is not signed by a judge, you are not required to let them inside.
  6. If the officers or agents force their way into your home, do not resist. You may tell them: “You are not allowed to enter. I do not consent to a search. I wish to remain silent. I wish to speak with an attorney as soon as possible.”
If you are arrested:
  1. Do not attempt to resist arrest
  2. You have the right to request to speak with an attorney.
  3. You have the right to request an interpreter.
  4. Remember, you do not have to say anything or answer any questions before speaking with an attorney
  5. You have the right to consult with an immigration attorney before making any decisions about your case or signing any documents. No one can force you to make a final decision or sign a document if you do not fully understand it.
